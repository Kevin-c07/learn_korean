한 Hangul — the Korean Alphabet

Hangul has 14 basic consonants and 10 basic vowels. Unlike Chinese, it is fully phonetic!

Why Hangul is Amazing

Hangul was invented in 1443 by King Sejong the Great specifically so ordinary people could learn to read and write. Each symbol represents a sound, and letters are combined into syllable blocks. You can learn to read it in just a few hours!

📖 Intermediate Korean

Key Grammar Concepts

Korean is an SOV language (Subject-Object-Verb) — the verb always comes last. Particles attach to nouns to mark their role. Formality levels are essential — always know who you're speaking to!

Two Politeness Levels

Formal polite (합쇼체): Use with strangers, elders, bosses. Verbs end in -ㅂ니다 / -습니다.

Informal polite (해요체): Everyday speech with adults. Verbs end in -아요 / -어요.

Casual (반말): Close friends, younger people only. Dropping the 요.
